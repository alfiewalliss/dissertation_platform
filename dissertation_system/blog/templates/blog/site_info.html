Site Information

Our Platform

What is the aim of Dissertation Exchange?

The aim of Dissertation Exchange is to provide a centralised platform for students throughout the UK to publish their work. We understand that the process of uploading work to other journals can be time-consuming and tedious. Student-led research is often discarded once the project or assessment is complete, despite its potential to contribute to academia. Dissertation Exchange aims to make student-led research accessible while maintaining academic credibility through a quality peer-review system. Our goal is to create a friendly environment where students can interact with one another to discuss student-produced literature.

Peer Review

Who can review your post?

At Dissertation Exchange, anyone who is verified can review posts, provided they have at least one profile tag that matches a tag associated with the paper they wish to evaluate. If a user is not verified or lacks matching tags, they can still share their opinions in the comments section. We encourage all users to be constructive and respectful when leaving comments. Our aim is to foster a community that values open and honest feedback while maintaining a supportive environment for all users.

What is the peer review process?

The goal of Dissertation Exchange is to bridge the gap between academic credibility and accessibility. To achieve this, we have streamlined the peer review process, making it simple yet effective. Reviewers fill out a form that provides feedback on the paper and then render a verdict on its academic value. Once the form is submitted, the author of the paper will receive an email containing the review verdict, as well as the notes made by the reviewer. Papers that pass the process will be marked as academically credible, while those that fail will be marked as unreliable. If your paper does not pass the process, you can attempt to get it reviewed again after implementing the relevant feedback provided by the original reviewer. Our peer review process is designed to increase the volume of quality, peer-reviewed literature on our site, making it an excellent resource for students and researchers alike.

How can I apply for my paper to be peer review?

At Dissertation Exchange, we do not require an application form for peer review of your paper. Reviewers are free to select any work that interests them and has matching tags. If you want to increase interest in your paper, you can directly message a verified user with matching tags to inquire if they would be willing to review it. A verified user has the symbol "" on their profile. This is the best way to get your paper noticed by potential reviewers and increase your chances of getting it reviewed. Our platform is designed to facilitate the peer review process and help students and researchers receive constructive feedback from their peers.

Tags

What are tags?

We understand you may have some questions about tags and how they work on our platform. Tags are labels that help categorize users and posts so that people can search for and discover content relevant to their interests. For example, if someone tags their post as 'mythology', then a user looking for mythology-related content can look for posts with that tag.
Tags can be applied to posts and users. Posts must have one primary tag, but they can have multiple secondary tags. On the other hand, users can have zero or many tags. If a user has a tag that matches a post's tag, then the user is able to peer review the post. This is a great way to ensure quality and accuracy in the content posted.

Verification

What does being verified mean?

At Dissertation Exchange, being verified means that a user has gone through a verification process, which enables them to review papers with tags matching their profile. We have implemented this process to ensure a fair and unbiased review process. We want to prevent any possibility of unfair or unhelpful reviews, which could be left by trolls or those who lack the necessary expertise in the subject area of the paper. By verifying users, we aim to maintain the quality of our peer review process and ensure that all reviews are constructive, relevant, and helpful to the authors.

How can you become verified?

At Dissertation Exchange, we aim to keep the verification process simple to encourage more users to become verified, which, in turn, increases the volume of peer-reviewed literature on our platform. Verified users have the ability to promote other users to also become verified. It is crucial that verified users only promote users they believe are trustworthy and responsible, and who will not violate the trust placed upon them. The promotion of users to verified status is an irreversible process. At Dissertation Exchange, we operate on a trust-based system and require all users to act responsibly. If you are unverified and wish to become verified, you should message a verified user, preferably in your subject field, and explain why they should promote you. If they believe that you are trustworthy and knowledgeable in the relevant subject area, they may choose to promote you.
